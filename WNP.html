World-Newspapers.com

An unsolicited redesign

This was a project meant to test my understanding of vanilla JS, as well as to build something functional with a distinct style. The lack of styling on the original site was evident. It was functional, but not particularly useful. My goal was to make this a website that people would willingly visit in order to find some new, interesting news content from around the globe.

    01What & Why 02Problem Statement 03User Research 04Wireframes 05Responsive Protoypes

About The Project

What

Long story short, this website needed some help. The idea behind the redesign was to take a website that has some semblance of funcionality, and simply make it better. I found the original World-Newspapers.com to be interesting, yet not particularly stylish or useful. I wanted to change that by introducing RSS feeds into the page, and parsing them to display randomized articles for people to link out to. The new website will be organized by topic, while also preserving the functionality of the original site by prodiving links to newspapers all around the world.

Context

Why

There are a ton of websites dedicated to delivering the news, so why make another one? Well, this project is designed to achieve something slightly different than what most news sites aim for. I wanted a website that will be infinitely interesting, yet not overwhelming like some of the competitors. This is a website for people to come and simply browse, with no particular agenda in mind. This redesign will provide people with a stream of endlessly interesting, bipartisan news articles from around the globe. It is built for the people who are constantly curious, and happily distracted.

Building

Wireframe for Desktop

Designed and Developed in Adobe XD, with responsiveness in mind. The breakpoints were considered as I developed the 12 column layout defined by Bootstrap 4. The sections are designed to stack in order of importance as the page shrinks in size.

Key Takeaways

Conslusion

This project was particularly satisfying to work on because of the complexity of the code. I learned a significant amount about organizing and styling RSS feeds within a JS file, and how they act on a live webpage. The functionality of the website was fun to build in, and taught me that all usually it takes to debug jQuery and JS is research and persistence. After this, I am much more confident in my ability to make a website clean and funtional with repeatable code snips.
